Lovely Morning Porridge

Recipe

More often than not, porridge is made with a base of oats (though, you can substitute with other grains such as quinoa or rice). Whole oats are high in fibre and one of the only sources of a unique antioxidant called avenanthramides, which a 2015 Harvard University study found significantly reduces your risk of heart disease. They're super filling, thanks to the slow-release carbs, and warming for when the English weather decides to do what it does best (read: get cold, dark and rainy). Alongside cereal and toast, porridge is the most popular healthy breakfast meal in the UK â€” so much so that porridge recipes (and even Instagrams) are in abundance. And sometimes we even find ourselves shirking avo toast for it at brunch.

Ingredients

  1. 100g of oats
  2. Whole apple
  3. 1 tbsp of cinnamon

Directions

  1. Put the oats in the fridge with 200ml of milk to soften overnight
  2. Next morning chop up the whole apple to small bitesize pieces. Do the same with cinnamon - cover the apple in cinnamon.
  3. Put everything together and start boiling on the stove. After everything is soft and ready take the pot of the stove.
  4. Serve with additional apple pieces and cinnamon.

    5. Enjoy!
